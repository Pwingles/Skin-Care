The Schedule

Weekly Skincare Calendar

The sequence and frequency of each treatment matters as much as the ingredients themselves. This is the exact weekly framework from the protocol, structured around skin cycling principles.

Morning Routine — Daily

Step Product / Action Frequency Notes
01 — Cleanse Foaming or hydrating cleanser (skin-type matched) Optional (cleanse if oily or post-workout) Night cleanse is mandatory. Morning only if needed.
02 — Vitamin C L-ascorbic acid 15–20% + Vitamin E + Ferulic acid Daily Apply to dry skin. Wait 60–90 seconds before next step.
03 — Azelaic Acid Paula's Choice 10% (or prescription 15–20%) Daily (if pigmentation concern) Skip if no dark spots or redness. Apply after Vitamin C.
04 — Niacinamide La Roche-Posay Mela B3 or The Ordinary 10% Daily Can be skipped if using CeraVe PM as moisturizer (contains 4%).
05 — Moisturizer CeraVe Cream (dry) or LRP Toleriane (oily) Daily Apply to slightly damp skin for best barrier support.
06 — SPF La Roche-Posay Anthelios Mineral SPF 50+ When outdoors 10am–4pm Skip if fully indoors. Early morning sun (pre-10am) is fine without SPF.

The Fine Details

Micro-Optimisations

Small changes that don't feel like skincare — but quietly compound over months and years.

01
Copper Pillowcase

An RCT found sleeping on a copper-oxide pillowcase (≥2% copper) reduced facial wrinkles measurably at 2 and 4 weeks. 7–8 hours of nightly contact compounds over years. Prevents sleep lines from becoming permanent creases.

Shop Copper Pillowcases
02
Fresh Face Towel

By day 3–4, a face towel has accumulated oil, bacteria, and dead skin cells. Replace your face towel every 2–3 uses — or use disposable cotton face cloths. This directly impacts the bacteria load transferred to freshly cleansed skin.

Shop Face Cloths
03
Bedroom Environment

Optimal bedroom: 20–22°C (68–72°F), 40–60% humidity. CO₂ below 500ppm (crack a window for 10 minutes morning and night). High CO₂ disrupts deep sleep — less deep sleep means less skin repair. A HEPA + activated carbon air purifier if near urban areas or wildfire zones.
