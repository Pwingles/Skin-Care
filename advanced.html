Beyond the Surface

Beneath the
Skin

The surface routine protects and repairs. These three treatments go deeper — physically stimulating your skin to rebuild collagen and reverse aging from within. This is what separates good skin from extraordinary skin.

Why Advanced Treatments

The limitation of topicals

Every topical skincare product has a fundamental ceiling — most can only reach the epidermis, the outermost skin layer. They hydrate, protect, and repair the surface. But they cannot penetrate deep enough into the dermis to stimulate meaningful collagen remodeling or muscle maintenance.

Advanced treatments bypass this limit entirely. They work through controlled injury, photobiomodulation, and electrical stimulation — triggering your body's own deep repair mechanisms. These are the only methods clinically proven to physically reverse visible aging, not just slow it.

Treatment 01

Red Light Therapy

One of the few advanced treatments with serious, replicated research behind it. Red light (photobiomodulation) uses specific wavelengths to penetrate deep into the dermis, stimulating mitochondria in your skin cells to produce more energy — which accelerates collagen synthesis, reduces inflammation, and speeds cellular repair.

Critically: it doesn't work the same way as topicals. While retinoids stimulate collagen from the epidermis downward, red light stimulates it from within the dermis outward — making them genuinely complementary, not redundant.

  • 2–3 sessions per week, 10–15 minutes each
  • 48–72 hours minimum rest between sessions
  • Daily use can increase oxidative stress — counterproductive
  • Allow 8–12 weeks for measurable collagen changes
The Study

Participants using red light masks twice weekly for 3 months showed: 38.3% reduction in crow's feet wrinkles, 24.8% reduction in skin sagging, 32.8% reduction in pore size, and 34.9% improvement in skin homogeneity. All key markers of skin health.

Effective wavelengths:

630–660nm — Surface collagen
810–880nm — Deeper dermis penetration
Amplification Protocol — Apply Before Session
Copper Peptides

When combined with red light in human fibroblast studies, collagen synthesis increased by 70% and growth factors by 230% compared to red light alone. Apply copper peptide serum to clean skin 2–3 minutes before your session.

Green Tea Extract

A pilot study applying green tea-soaked pads for 20 minutes before a red light session showed measurable wrinkle and crow's feet reduction at 4 weeks. Small study, but low-risk to test if you have green tea available.

Treatment 02

Microneedling

One of the most effective treatments for acne scars, deep wrinkles, and skin texture. Tiny needles create controlled punctures that trigger a wound-healing cascade — flooding the area with growth factors, collagen, and elastin to rebuild the dermis from the inside out.

Unlike topicals that sit on the surface and unlike red light that uses energy, microneedling physically forces your dermis to regenerate. This is why it produces results topicals simply cannot replicate.

  • Do not microneedle over active acne or open wounds
  • Always sanitize your device before every session
  • Start at lower depths — build tolerance first
  • Real collagen remodeling takes 4–6 weeks post-session
  • Wear sunscreen for 5–7 days post-treatment, even indoors
  • 3–6 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart for significant scar improvement
The Study

A 12-study meta-analysis found microneedling at 1.5mm depth reduced acne scars by 62% and increased collagen density by 400%. Results compound significantly across multiple sessions over 6+ months.

Treatment 03

Facial EMS

Electrical Muscle Stimulation. Both red light and microneedling address the dermis. EMS addresses something they can't — the muscle layer beneath the skin.

As you age, facial muscles thin out, weaken, and lose tone. When that scaffolding weakens, even healthy skin begins to sag. EMS uses low-level electrical pulses to contract and strengthen facial muscles — like resistance training for your face — lifting the tissue sitting on top of them.

This is most relevant for targeting visible sagging, jawline definition, and volume loss — concerns that no topical or collagen-stimulating treatment alone can address.

  • 5 sessions per week, 20 minutes per session
  • Do not exceed 20 minutes per session
  • Target all four primary zones in each session
  • Results compound over 8–12 weeks of consistent use
The Study

A clinical study with participants using facial EMS five times per week found: 17% increase in facial muscle thickness, measurable reduction in facial sagging, and improved jawline definition. The mechanism is muscle strengthening, not skin tightening.

Target Zones:

Cheeks
Jawline

Around the eyes

Do not use EMS if: you have a pacemaker, electrical implants, metal plates in your face, active skin infections, or open wounds.
